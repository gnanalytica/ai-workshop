Yesterday you defined a problem on assumptions. Today you go find out if those assumptions survive contact with a real human. Most don't.

## 🎯 Today's objective

**Topic.** Running user interviews that produce *signal*, not flattery — using The Mom Test rules and the Jobs-to-be-Done lens.

**By end of class you will have:**
1. Run one 10-minute live interview, recorded and AI-transcribed.
2. Extracted one Jobs-to-be-Done statement: *"When [situation], I want [motivation], so I can [outcome]."*
3. A clear feel for **past behaviour vs hypothetical opinion** — and why only the first one matters.

> *Why this matters.* Your capstone tomorrow needs *evidence-backed* problem locking. The interviews you do today and tonight are that evidence.

> 🧠 **Quick glossary.** **Mom Test** = ask about life, not your idea. **Compliment** = noise. **Hypothetical** ("would you use…") = noise. **Specific past behaviour** = signal. **JTBD** = the job the user "hires" your product to do.

### The 3 Mom Test rules

1. **Talk about their life, not your idea.** Never pitch.
2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not opinions about the future.** "Walk me through the last time…" beats "would you use…"
3. **Talk less, listen more.** Aim for 80/20 — they talk, you listen.

## 🧪 Lab: Live interview + JTBD extraction

1. **Set up recording.** Open Otter/Fireflies/Read.AI. Get verbal consent before recording.
2. **Interview (10 min).** Use only Mom-Test-shaped questions. Suggested openers:
   - "Walk me through the last time you faced [problem]."
   - "What did you do right after that?"
   - "How are you currently solving this?"
3. **Transcribe.** Let the AI tool generate the transcript. Verify timestamps.
4. **Extract with AI (5 min).** Paste transcript into ChatGPT with the prompt of the day. Mark compliments, hypotheticals, and signal.
5. **Write JTBD (3 min).** *"When [situation], I want [motivation], so I can [outcome]."*

**Artifact.** A Google Doc with: (a) raw transcript link, (b) 3 verbatim signal quotes, (c) one JTBD statement, (d) one question you should have asked but didn't. Submit on dashboard.

> ⚠️ **Privacy.** Always get consent. Don't record minors. Strip identifying info before sharing.
