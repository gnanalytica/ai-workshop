### What AI **is** ✓
1. A **pattern machine** — saw trillions of sentences, finishes yours by matching patterns.
2. A **brilliant, under-slept intern** — fast, creative, occasionally makes things up with total confidence.
3. A **force multiplier** — 1 hour now does what used to take 1 week, *if* you can direct it.
4. **Already everywhere** — Maps, Netflix, UPI fraud, Gmail spam, Instagram feed.
5. **Stateless by default** — every chat starts fresh unless the app gives it memory.

### What AI **isn't** ✗
1. **Not conscious** — no feelings, no desires.
2. **Not always right** — confidently wrong 15–30% of the time. That's *hallucination*. Always verify.
3. **Not thinking** like you — it predicts the next word. Math, not reasoning.
4. **Not a judgment replacement** — can draft your SOP; shouldn't pick your career.
5. **Not a search engine** — search finds what exists; AI *generates* what sounds right.

## 🧪 Lab: Same prompt, four brains

Open ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok in four tabs.

1. Copy this prompt: *"You are my college placement mentor. I'm a 3rd-year CSE student with a 7.8 CGPA and one internship at a startup. List the 5 highest-leverage things I should do in the next 90 days."*
2. Paste into each. Save to a Google Doc with one section per model.
3. Compare on three axes: **specificity, tone, length**.
4. End with one sentence: *"The model that understood me best was ___ because ___."*

**Artifact.** One Google Doc with four answers + your take. Drop the share link in the cohort channel.

> ⚠️ **If a tool blocks you** — try a different Google account, use mobile hotspot for that tab, or note the blocker and continue with the other three.

## 📊 Live poll

**Which of the four felt most human?** ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini / Grok / *they all felt the same*. Your instructor will open this mid-lab and call on one voter from each side.

## 💬 Discuss

- Which model *felt* most human — and what specific behaviour triggered that? (Not "vibes.")
- Name one place AI has been in your life that you never labelled "AI."
- Name one task you'd still trust a human over any AI for. Defend it.
- If AI is wrong 20% of the time, where in your life is that *already* acceptable, and where is it absolutely not?
