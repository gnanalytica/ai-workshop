Section 2

Your teaching day — before, during, after

Every content/day-XX.md follows: 🎯 Objective → ⏪ Pre-class → 🎥 In-class → 🧪 Lab → 📊 Poll → 💬 Discuss → ❓ Quiz → 📝 Assignment → 🔁 Prep next → 📚 References.

⏪ Before class

  • Open day.html?day=N 24h ahead; read end-to-end.
  • Drop run-of-show into private prep notes; one-line greeting into the shared note (see §3).
  • Run the lab once locally; verify poll & quiz copy.
  • Confirm pod list, room/Zoom link, and your day_faculty slot.

🎥 During class

  • Lead drives objective, demo, lab walkthrough; launches poll & quiz at the marked beats.
  • Support marks attendance live, sweeps stuck queue ~every 10 min, watches for stuck body language.
  • Moderate Discuss: one prompt, two minutes, 2–3 students share.

🔁 After class

  • Trainers: grade prior day's assignment by 21:00 IST.
  • Lead: append "what landed / what didn't" to private prep notes.
  • Support: close resolved stuck items; escalate anything >24h stale.
  • Spot-check attendance before midnight.
Section 3

Using day.html — Faculty prep panel

On day.html?day=N, faculty assigned to that day see a Faculty prep panel between the header and Objective. Students never see it.

  • Private prep notes — faculty-only scratchpad: run-of-show, time-boxes, watch-outs. Sign additions with initials.
  • Shared note — one-line sticky shown to all students above the Objective. Lead writes this before 09:00 IST.

Both fields autosave 800ms after typing stops ("Saved ✓" badge). Both Lead and Support can edit; edits scope to the selected cohort.

Section 6

Grading workflow — trainers only

Each day's 📝 Assignment carries an inline rubric. Submissions due 21:00 IST next day. Open day.html?day=N → Submissions panel → click a row.

Score every criterion with one short actionable comment. Save publishes the grade instantly. Flag borderlines in the comment rather than guessing.

Milestone days (6, 11, 16, 21, 26, 29, 30) add a peer-review step before trainer finalizes. The trainer's score is ground truth — watch for peer inflation.

Lead and Support cannot open the grading drawer (UI + RLS). If Support has rubric input, leave a stuck-queue comment tagged grading-input. Full role matrix: see §1.

Section 7

Triage flowchart

⚠️ Red flags — skip triage, phone Trainer + Admin now
  • Student in distress — panic, tears, harassment reports.
  • Academic-integrity concern — impersonation, paid completion, leaked answers.
  • Platform-wide outage — Supabase down, errors across the pod.
  • Misuse / policy breach — unsafe generated content, repeated CoC violations.
  • Anything outside your judgment. Escalating is the right call.

For everything else, walk each diamond in order.

Section 12

Code of conduct & emergencies

Community. Every question is legitimate. No sarcasm, public shaming, or "you should know this". Faculty disagreements stay between faculty — never debate curriculum in front of the cohort.

Inclusion. Gender-neutral defaults; mirror students' pronouns. Honour name/pronoun updates the same hour.

Academic integrity. Generative tools are encouraged; verbatim peer copies are not. On suspicion of impersonation, document and escalate to the Trainer — do not confront.

Emergencies. Physical safety: local emergency services first, then Admin. Platform outages: Tech Support (§9). Harassment/distress: pause, move to a private space, escalate to Admin within the hour.

Confidentiality. Stuck posts, prep notes, and grading comments stay inside the platform. No external screenshots of student submissions without consent.
