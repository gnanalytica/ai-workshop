## 🎮 The Say-What-You-See game

[Say What You See](https://artsandculture.google.com/experiment/say-what-you-see/jwG3m7wQShZngw) is a Google Arts & Culture experiment that turns prompting into a game.

**How it works.**
1. The site shows you an image (a painting, a photo, an artwork).
2. You write a description in your own words.
3. An AI redraws the image **from your description**.
4. You see how close the redrawn image is to the original.

**Why we play it.** It's the cleanest, most honest test of *how clear your prompts are*. Vague descriptions produce vague redraws. Specific, well-ordered descriptions produce close matches. It's CREATE, but visual.

**Class rules.**
- Play **5 rounds** individually. Note your best score and your worst.
- After each round, write **one sentence** about what changed when you added a specific detail.
- The top 3 scorers share their winning description aloud.

**Tips while you play.**
- Describe **shapes, colours, positions, and relationships** — not feelings.
- Order matters: start with the big subject, then add details.
- "A red apple on a wooden table, sunlight from the left" beats "an apple in a nice setting."

## 🧪 Lab 1: Say-What-You-See

**In class (3 rounds, ~15 min).** Open https://artsandculture.google.com/experiment/say-what-you-see/jwG3m7wQShZngw and play 3 rounds together. For each round, jot down: *image subject in 3 words · your description · score · what you'd change next time*.

After round 2, deliberately try the opposite of round 1 — if you went short, go long; if you went generic, get hyper-specific. See which scores higher.

**At home (2 more rounds).** Finish to a total of 5 rounds and end with one sentence: *"My descriptions improve when I ___."*

**Artifact.** A 5-row table in your M1 Doc showing all 5 rounds. This is your warm-up before the capstone work.

## 🧪 Lab 2 (main): Capstone Milestone 1

After the game, we shift to your capstone. Open your `<Your Name> – Capstone M1` Doc.

**In class (~15 min):**
1. **Section A — 10 ideas.** Use the prompt-of-the-day with a domain you care about. Run it on **two** different models (ChatGPT + Claude). Paste both lists. *(~8 min)*
2. **Section B — Narrow to 1.** Score each idea on three axes: *I've lived this problem (1–5)*, *paying customer is obvious (1–5)*, *AI clearly helps (1–5)*. Pick the highest total. *(~4 min)*
3. **Section E — One-line pitch.** Draft a first version: *"For [user] who [pain], [product] is a [category] that [magic], unlike [alternative]."* *(~3 min)*

**At home (before Sunday 23:59 IST):**

4. **Section C — Problem proof.** Write a 100-word lived-experience story. No market jargon — the moment you felt the pain.
5. **Section D — 5 cited sources.** Use Perplexity to find 5 real sources backing the problem (size, frequency, who suffers).
6. Sharpen Section E based on Sections C and D.
7. Share the Doc with edit access to your pod faculty.

**Artifact.** Submitted Google Doc with sections A–E filled. Drop the link in the **#capstone-m1** channel before **Sunday 23:59 IST**.
