> 🏁 **Milestone day.** This is the first of four capstone checkpoints. Submitting M1 unlocks Week 2's labs. Skip it and you fall behind on demo eligibility.

Five days in, you've used four chatbots, cracked open a transformer, learned CREATE, met Sarvam, and run Deep Research. Today you compete in a prompt game in the morning and **start your capstone** in the afternoon. By Sunday night, you'll have a problem worth solving for the next 24 days.

## 🎯 Today's objective

**Topic.** Prompt game + Capstone Milestone 1: ideathon, brainstorm, research.

**Milestone deliverable.** A 2-page Capstone M1 brief in a shared Google Doc, by end of weekend.

**By end of class you will have:**
1. Played a 30-min team **prompt game** — same problem, best CREATE prompt wins.
2. Brainstormed **10 capstone ideas** with AI in 15 minutes.
3. Narrowed to **1 real problem** you've personally lived.
4. Backed it with **5 cited sources** (Perplexity / Deep Research / NotebookLM).

> *Why this matters.* Ideas don't fall from the sky. The best capstones in past cohorts came from students who solved a problem in *their own hostel, their own family, their own placement struggle*. Today is that pick.

### The prompt game (morning)

- 3-person teams. Same problem statement (e.g., *"Help a Tier-2 student plan a 90-day placement push"*).
- Each team has 20 min to write **one** CREATE prompt and **one** few-shot example.
- Outputs judged blind on **specificity, usefulness, tone**.
- Winning prompt becomes the cohort's reference.

### The capstone arc

- **M1 (today).** Problem + 1 idea + 5 sources.
- **M2 (Day 12).** Wireframes + first AI prototype.
- **M3 (Day 20).** Working demo + 3 user interviews.
- **M4 (Day 28).** Polish + pitch + demo-day rehearsal.

## 🧪 Lab: Capstone Milestone 1

1. Open your `<Your Name> – Capstone M1` Doc.
2. Section **A — 10 ideas.** Use the prompt-of-the-day with a domain you care about. Generate 10 ideas with **two** different models. Paste both lists.
3. Section **B — Narrow to 1.** Score each on: *I've lived this problem (1–5)*, *paying customer is obvious (1–5)*, *AI clearly helps (1–5)*. Pick the highest total.
4. Section **C — Problem proof.** Write a 100-word lived-experience story. No market jargon — the moment you felt the pain.
5. Section **D — 5 cited sources.** Use Perplexity / Deep Research / NotebookLM to find 5 real sources backing the problem (size, frequency, who suffers).
6. Section **E — One-line pitch.** *"For [user] who [pain], [product] is a [category] that [magic], unlike [alternative]."*
7. Share the Doc with edit access to your mentor pod.

**Artifact.** Submitted Google Doc with sections A–E filled. Drop the link in the **#capstone-m1** channel before Sunday 23:59 IST.
