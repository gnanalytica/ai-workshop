> 🚩 **Milestone 2.** Graded checkpoint. In Milestone 2 you transform your validated idea into a tangible prototype and deliver a pitch. The two core deliverables are a **slide deck** and a **recorded product demo**, backed by a working **prototype**.

## 🎯 Learning objectives

- Convert an idea into a working prototype.
- Structure a compelling **4–8 minute pitch**.
- Record and narrate a product demo.
- Communicate problem, solution, and impact clearly.

## 📦 Deliverables

1. **Slide deck** — 6–10 slides (PDF or shareable link).
2. **Demo recording** — hosted on Vimeo, Loom, or YouTube Unlisted.
3. **Prototype** — vibe-coded or no-code; submit a live link or a walkthrough video.

**Key themes:** Prototyping · Storytelling · Demo recording · Pitch structure · Vibe coding · User journey.

## ⏪ Pre-class · ~45–60 min

### What to do before class

- [ ] **Watch 2 Y Combinator Demo Day pitches** on YouTube. Note what hooks you in the first 30 seconds. *(~20 min)*
- [ ] **Re-read your Milestone 1 submission** to refresh your problem statement and solution idea.
- [ ] **Explore one prototyping tool** — Bolt, Lovable, Framer, or v0 — and pick the one that fits your product best. *(~15 min)*
- [ ] **Draft a rough 6-slide outline** using this structure: **Problem → Solution → How it works → Demo → Impact → Next steps.** *(~15 min)*

### Reflection prompts

- What is the single most important thing your audience should understand about your product?
- What does the core user journey look like in **3 steps**?
- What would "done" look like for your prototype by end of this week?

## 🎥 In-class · 75 minutes

| Block | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| Recap | 0–10 min | Milestone 1 recall — problem statements, target users, solution concepts. Bridge from idea validation to prototype building. |
| Pitch anatomy | 10–25 min | Walk through the 4–8 min pitch formula slide by slide. Sample pitch reference. One-idea-per-slide rule. Using product screenshots as slide backgrounds. |
| Vibe coding live demo | 25–45 min | Instructor live-builds a simple prototype in Bolt or Lovable. Students then attempt their own. |
| Demo recording practice | 45–60 min | Students record a rough 2-minute walkthrough of their prototype in Loom. Pairs review each other using the peer-review rubric. |
| Q&A + checklist | 60–75 min | Review the Milestone 2 submission checklist together. Address blockers. Confirm submission format and due date. |

### The 6-slide spine

**Problem → Solution → How it works → Demo → Impact → Next steps.** One idea per slide. Product screenshots as backgrounds where possible.

### Vibe coding — the live build

The instructor live-builds an MVP UI from a single prompt in Bolt or Lovable to show how quickly a usable surface can be generated. Then you do yours.

## ✅ Post-class · Submission checklist

- [ ] Slide deck ready as a shareable link (Drive/PDF link).
- [ ] Demo recording link ready (Vimeo / Loom / YouTube Unlisted).
- [ ] Prototype is accessible via live link **or** walkthrough video.
- [ ] Pitch is **4–8 minutes** including demo.
- [ ] Problem and solution are clearly communicated.
- [ ] At least **one user scenario** is shown in the demo.

**Submit.** Dashboard → Day 19 → 3 links (slide deck, demo recording, prototype).
