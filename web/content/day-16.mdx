> 🏆 **Milestone 3.** This is a graded checkpoint. Every team presents. Every team gets feedback. There's no "we're not ready yet" — what you have today is what you have. Polish the demo of *what works*, don't fake what doesn't.

Today is the first time you'll feel what Day 30 feels like. Same format, lower stakes. Use it.

## 🎯 Today's objective

**Topic.** Capstone Milestone 3 — Presentation, Pitch, Prototype, Mini Demo.

**By end of class you will have:**
1. Delivered a 5-minute pitch with the deck you built this week.
2. Run a 2-minute *live* demo (no recordings, no "imagine if").
3. Received written feedback from 2 faculty + 3 peers on the rubric below.

> *Why this matters.* You'll iterate the demo for two more weeks. Honest feedback now beats polite feedback at the finale.

### Primer (~10 min)

- **Read:** YC's *How to pitch your startup* — https://www.ycombinator.com/library/6m-how-to-pitch-your-startup
- **Read:** Guy Kawasaki's *10/20/30 rule* — https://guykawasaki.com/the_102030_rule/

### Bring to class

- [ ] Printed copy of your slide notes (1 page max).
- [ ] HDMI/USB-C dongle for projector.

> 🧠 **Quick glossary.** **Demo** = you click, it works, in front of humans. **Walkthrough** = you narrate Figma. We want **demos**. **Cold open** = first 30 seconds of your pitch — make them count.

## 🎥 In-class · live session

### Agenda

| Block | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| Format reminder + order | 10 min | Slot order on the board |
| Group A presentations | 60 min | 6 teams × 10 min each |
| Break | 15 min | Snacks + cohort feedback forms |
| Group B presentations | 60 min | 6 teams × 10 min each |
| Faculty debrief | 15 min | Common patterns we saw |

### The 5-2-3 format

- **5 min pitch.** Problem → user → solution → why-now → demo lead-in → ask.
- **2 min live demo.** Click, don't narrate. If it crashes, recover, don't apologise.
- **3 min Q&A.** Faculty + 2 peer questions. Short answers win.

### Rules

1. **Hard cut at 7 min.** Mic off. We protect the next team's slot.
2. **No "in the future we'll…"** in the pitch. Save it for Q&A if asked.
3. **Demo from the deployed URL**, not localhost. Preview links from Bolt/Vercel are fine.
4. **One slide of numbers** is mandatory: users you've talked to, signups, anything real.

## 🧪 Lab: Live milestone presentations

1. **30 min before your slot:** open your deck + demo, close every other tab, mute notifications.
2. **2 min before:** plug in, share screen, sip water.
3. **Pitch.** Eyes up. Don't read slides.
4. **Demo.** Narrate the *user's* journey, not your code.
5. **Q&A.** "I don't know yet, here's how I'd find out" beats making things up.
6. **After:** fill out self-reflection form on the dashboard while it's fresh.

**Artifact.** Recorded video of your pitch (your team-mate films on a phone, landscape) + the filled rubric. Upload both before midnight.

## 📊 Live poll

**Right after each pitch, the cohort votes:** *Would you use this product today?* Yes / Maybe / Need more info / No / Not the target user.

## 💬 Discuss (after both groups)

- Which team's *problem statement* was sharpest, and what made it sharp?
- Whose demo had the cleanest cold open? Steal that move.
- What was the most common weak spot across all teams? (Faculty will share theirs too.)

## ❓ Quiz

No quiz today — the pitch *is* the assessment.

## 📝 Assignment · Iteration plan

**Brief.** Within 24 hours, write a 200-word *iteration plan* answering: (a) one thing the feedback got right that hurt to hear, (b) one thing you'll change before Day 24, (c) one assumption you'll go test with real users this week.

**Submit.** Paste on the dashboard. Faculty mentors will reply inline.

**Rubric (Milestone 3).** Problem clarity (4) · Working live demo (4) · Real-world numbers / user evidence (2).
