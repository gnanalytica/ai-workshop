Goal: Move from "guessing" to "knowing."

The Big Idea: Having a great product (Product Fit) doesn't matter if nobody needs it (Market Fit).
What you'll learn: How to identify your "ideal" user and talk to them in a way that reveals the truth about your business idea.

## ⏪ Pre-class

Goal: Preparation and Setup.

User Persona: Create a profile for your target customer (e.g., "Real Estate Sarah"). What is her role? What is her biggest daily headache?
JTBD (Jobs To Be Done): Define the "Job" they are hiring a solution for.
Example: They aren't buying "valuation software"; they are hiring a way to "look professional and save time."

AI Roleplay: Use an LLM to simulate a skeptical customer. Practice your questions here first to find gaps in your logic.

## 🎥 In-class · live session

Goal: Conducting the Interview.

The Mom Test: Ask about past behaviors, not future promises.
Bad: "Would you use this?"
Good: "How did you handle this problem last week?"

The 80/20 Rule: Listen 80% of the time. Let the user tell their story without interrupting to "sell" your idea.
Openers: Use phrases like "Tell me about the last time..." or "Walk me through your process for..."

## 📝 Assignment · Analysis and Action

Goal: Analysis and Action.

Empathy Mapping: Categorize your notes into four buckets: What did the user Say, Do, Think, and Feel?
Identify Patterns: If 3 out of 5 users complained about the same step in their process, that is your "Golden Opportunity."
User Journey Roadmap: Draw a simple map of how a user goes from "having a problem" to "using your product" to "solving the problem."

## 📚 References

Goal: Deep Dive Resources.

Book: The Mom Test by Rob Fitzpatrick (The gold standard for interviews).
Framework: Competing Against Luck by Clayton Christensen (The foundation of JTBD).
Tools: Use AI models (like Gemini or ChatGPT) for rapid persona generation and sentiment analysis of your interview transcripts.
