You came back from Milestone 1 with an idea. Mentors left comments. Most read: *"too vague," "what exactly is the problem?", "who pays?"*. Today you fix that — in three passes, with three frameworks that have survived from DARPA labs to IDEO whiteboards.

## 🎯 Today's objective

**Topic.** Problem statements with Heilmeier, 5 Whys, How Might We.

**By end of class you will have:**
1. Run your M1 idea through the **Heilmeier Catechism** (9 questions) with AI as your tough guide.
2. Used **5 Whys** to find the *root cause* under your stated problem.
3. Generated **5 "How Might We"** reframes — picked one to anchor your build.
4. A **single-tweet problem statement** (≤280 chars) that your mentor would sign off on.

> *Why this matters.* Bad problem statement = right answer to wrong question = wasted 24 days. Heilmeier is how DARPA decides which $50M projects to fund. 5 Whys is how Toyota stopped factory fires. You're using both on a 30-day capstone — overkill is the point.

> 🧠 **Quick glossary.** **Heilmeier Catechism** = 9 questions every project must answer. **5 Whys** = ask "why?" five times to find the root cause. **How Might We (HMW)** = open-ended reframe of a problem into a designable challenge. **Problem statement** = one sentence describing user, pain, context. **Root cause** = the *real* reason, not the symptom.

### The three frameworks, side-by-side

- **Heilmeier (9 Qs).** What are you trying to do? How is it done today? What's new? Who cares? If successful, what difference? What are the risks? How much will it cost? How long? Mid- and final-success metrics?
- **5 Whys.** State problem → ask why → answer → ask why → repeat 5×. Stops at root cause, not symptoms.
- **How Might We.** Take the root cause. Reframe as *"How might we…"* — open enough for many solutions, narrow enough to act. Generate 5, pick 1.

## 🧪 Lab: Sharpen the M1 problem — 15 min, in this room

Five steps. Tight clock. Walk out with the artefact.

1. **Claude · 3 min.** Paste your M1 problem statement. Run the **prompt-of-the-day** — get all 9 Heilmeier answers in your Doc. Don't skip the awkward ones.
2. **ChatGPT · 3 min.** Run **5 Whys** from your symptom. *Stop at a system, not a person* — if you land on "X is lazy," drill one more why.
3. **Gemini (Thinking) · 3 min.** Prompt: *"Generate 5 'How Might We' reframes for this root cause. Each must be specific enough that a solution is testable in 7 days."* Pick **one**.
4. **Write the tweet · 4 min.** ≤280 chars, in the format: *"For [user] who [pain in their words], we're building [solution] so they can [outcome] — measured by [metric]."* Paste back into Claude: *"Critique this in 3 bullets like a tough VC."* Rewrite once.
5. **Post · 2 min.** Update M1 Doc with new section **F — Sharpened problem statement**. Drop the tweet into **#capstone-m1-sharpened**.

**Walk-out artefact.** 1 Heilmeier scorecard · 1 Whys chain · 3 HMWs · **1 tweet**. If you don't leave with the tweet, the class didn't happen for you.

### Bonus — for fast finishers (steel-man the critic)

Open a new Claude tab. Paste your final tweet. Prompt:

> *"You are the harshest possible mentor on my panel. Find the ONE thing wrong with this problem statement that would make the whole project fail in week 3. Be specific. No generic feedback."*

Then ask Claude for a single edit that fixes it. Paste the new tweet into your Doc with a 1-line note: *"Edit driven by adversarial critique."*
