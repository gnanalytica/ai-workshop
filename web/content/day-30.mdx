Day 30. Some of you walked in 30 days ago not sure what an LLM was. Today you ship a thing, on a clock, in front of strangers. That is the entire point.

## 🎯 Today's objective

**Topic.** Demo Day 2 + cohort closing.

**By end of class you will have:**
1. Either delivered your live demo or watched and judged the second half.
2. Published a portfolio page (LinkedIn post + personal site / GitHub README) linking your capstone.
3. Joined the alumni Slack and committed to one post-cohort build cadence.

> *Why this matters.* The cohort ends today. The work doesn't. The single biggest predictor of who keeps shipping after Day 30 is whether they joined the alumni network and posted by Sunday.

## ⏪ Pre-class · ~30 min

### Setup

- [ ] Same as Day 29: deployed capstone, 5-slide deck, backup recording, charger, hotspot.
- [ ] Draft of your LinkedIn post ready in Notes (publish *after* your demo).
- [ ] Personal site or GitHub README with capstone link, demo video slot, and one-line story.

### Primer

- **Watch:** Last cohort's closing reel — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hu4Yvq-g7_Y
- **Read:** Paul Graham, *How to do great work* — last three sections only.

### Bring to class

- [ ] Your Day-1 reflection. Re-read it once before class.
- [ ] One thank-you you want to say publicly during closing.

> 🧠 **Quick glossary.** **Portfolio page** = the URL a recruiter clicks first. **Alumni network** = the people who'll DM you about jobs in 2027. **Compounding** = the only AI career strategy that works.

## 🧪 Demo slot · the final run

This is your last 5 minutes on the cohort clock. Use them.

- Same structure as Day 29: cold open → problem → live demo → architecture → ask.
- Watch your tomorrow's audience: recruiters, alumni, and the next cohort sit in.
- Recording goes straight to your portfolio page.

**Artifact.** Demo recording on cohort drive + your live LinkedIn post + GitHub README updated by end of session.

> ⚠️ **Don't apologise on stage.** Not for missing features, not for jitters, not for v1. The audience came to see what you built, not what you didn't.

## 📊 Live poll · audience feedback + cohort vote

**After all demos, the cohort votes:** Most-shippable / Most-creative / Most-improved / Most-likely-to-actually-build-this-as-a-startup. Awards announced live.

## 💬 Discuss · closing circle

- One sentence: *"The version of me that walked in on Day 1 vs today."*
- One person you want to thank publicly.
- One thing you're going to keep shipping after Day 30.
- One thing you're going to stop doing.

## ❓ Quiz

Final post-demo pulse: *"Pick the demo (not yours) you'd most like to keep working on as a side-project."* + *"How likely are you to ship something in the next 14 days?"* (1–5). 60 seconds, anonymous.

## 📝 Assignment · Final portfolio + reflection

**Brief.** Publish three things, in order:
1. **LinkedIn post** — 150–250 words. Day-1 baseline → Day-30 capstone link. Tag the cohort. No humble-brag, no buzzwords.
2. **Portfolio page** — personal site or GitHub README with: capstone name, one-line, live link, demo video, tech stack, your contact.
3. **300-word final reflection** — *"What 30 days of AI actually changed."* Compare honestly with your Day-1 reflection. Submitted private to faculty.

**Submit.** Dashboard → Day 30 → 3 links (LinkedIn URL, portfolio URL, reflection upload).

**Rubric.** Specific Day-1 → Day-30 contrast (4) · Portfolio actually loads + demo plays (4) · Honest reflection, no LinkedIn-speak (2).

## 🔁 Prep · life after the cohort

Day 30 ends. Cohort doesn't.

- [ ] **Join the alumni Slack** — invite link shared in closing circle. Post your portfolio page in `#showcase` by Sunday.
- [ ] **Pick a build cadence** — one ship/week, one ship/month, or one big project/quarter. Post it in `#commitments`.
- [ ] **Pair up** — find one cohort-mate to be your weekly accountability buddy for the next 8 weeks.
- [ ] **Pay it forward** — alumni open office hours for the next cohort start in 4 weeks. Sign up to mentor one student.
- [ ] **Quarterly meetup** — first one is in 90 days, in person where the cohort hub is. Block your calendar.
