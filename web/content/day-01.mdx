> 🧠 **Quick glossary.** **AI** = umbrella for systems mimicking thinking. **LLM** = AI trained on text. **Chatbot** = app wrapper around an LLM. **Training data** = the text the LLM learned from. **Context window** = how much you + the model can keep in mind in one chat. **Hallucination** = AI confidently making up facts. **Prompt** = what you type.

### What AI **is** ✓
1. A **pattern machine** — saw trillions of sentences, finishes yours by matching patterns.
2. A **brilliant, under-slept intern** — fast, creative, occasionally makes things up with total confidence.
3. A **force multiplier** — 1 hour now does what used to take 1 week, *if* you can direct it.
4. **Already everywhere** — Maps, Netflix, UPI fraud, Gmail spam, Instagram feed.
5. **Stateless by default** — every chat starts fresh unless the app gives it memory.

### What AI **isn't** ✗
1. **Not conscious** — no feelings, no desires.
2. **Not always right** — confidently wrong 15–30% of the time. That's *hallucination*. Always verify.
3. **Not thinking** like you — it predicts the next word. Math, not reasoning.
4. **Not a judgment replacement** — can draft your SOP; shouldn't pick your career.
5. **Not a search engine** — search finds what exists; AI *generates* what sounds right.

## 🧪 Lab: Same prompt, four brains

Open ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok in four tabs.

1. Copy this prompt: *"You are my college placement mentor. I'm a 3rd-year CSE student with a 7.8 CGPA and one internship at a startup. List the 5 highest-leverage things I should do in the next 90 days."*
2. Paste into each.
3. Score each model on the **lab scorecard** below (1–5 stars per axis).
4. End with one sentence in your head: *"The model that understood me best was ___ because ___."*

### 🧮 Lab scorecard

> *Score outputs on what matters, not which brand you like.*

| Axis | What you're looking for | Low (★) | High (★★★★★) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Specificity** | Actual weeks, roles, companies, certifications. Names, not vibes. | "Build skills." "Network more." | "Weeks 1–2: finish CS50 final pset; week 3: 3 cold-DMs to Razorpay PM; week 4: AWS CCP voucher (₹0 with student pack)." |
| **Tone** | Friend, coach, consultant, or hype-bro? Pick what fits *you*. | Sycophantic ("Great question!"), vague mentor-speak, or fake-pumped. | Calm, opinionated, talks to *you* — not at a generic "student". |
| **Length** | Crisp plan or essay dump? More words ≠ more value. | 800-word essay restating the prompt before the answer. | A scannable list you can actually act on Monday. |
| **Safety / refusals** | Does it pushback, add caveats, name what it *won't* do? | Says yes to everything, no caveats, no "I don't know your college." | Asks one clarifier, flags one risk, names one thing it can't help with. |

**How to use it.** Don't average to a "winner." Look at the *shape* — one model might be ★★★★★ specificity but ★★ tone. That tells you *which model for which task*, not which brand to marry.

> ⚠️ **If a tool blocks you** — try a different Google account, use mobile hotspot for that tab, or note the blocker and continue with the other three.

## 📝 Assignment · Score-your-own prompt

**Brief.** Pick **one prompt of your own** — something *you actually want answered* (placement strategy, a study problem, a hostel decision, a side-project idea). Run it through all four models — **ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok** — and score each one using the lab scorecard.

**What to submit.**

1. **Your prompt** (paste it verbatim — exactly what you typed).
2. **Why this prompt** (one sentence — what real decision/output you wanted).
3. For **each of the four models**, a short block:
   - **Specificity** — ★/5 + one-line *why* (point at a concrete bit of the answer)
   - **Tone** — ★/5 + one-line *why*
   - **Length** — ★/5 + one-line *why*
   - **Safety / refusals** — ★/5 + one-line *why*
4. **Verdict.** *"For this prompt, I'd use ___ because ___."* One sentence. (Different from "the model that felt most human" — pick the one that *did the job*.)

**Format.** A simple table or four short blocks — whichever you prefer. Keep total length under one screen.

**Why this matters.** The in-class lab used my prompt. This one uses *yours* — so the scorecard becomes a habit you can run in 90 seconds anytime you're picking a model for a real task. By Day 30 you'll do this without thinking.

**Grading.** Submission credit only. Rubric: Prompt is real (2) · Scorecard filled for all 4 models (4) · Reasoning ties to specific output, not vibes (4).
